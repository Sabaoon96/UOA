Inflation in New Zealand

Everyone cares about how much things cost, and how the prices of goods and services change over time. Usually they increase, and this is called inflation. Recently, inflation has been low in many countries around the world, including New Zealand. Economists measure inflation using the consumer price index (CPI). Here is a graph and a table showing the percentage change in the CPI in recent quarters. Note: The graph goes further back in time than the table does.

Two of the last four quarters had negative inflation. Clearly houses aren't included in the CPI!
