Correlation is not causation

For a long time, statisticians have become frustrated when someone claims that a correlation (an observed relationship between two variables) necessarily implies that one of those variables causes the other one.

Some people have used humour to illustrate this point. For example, see this graph, showing the divorce rate in one state of the USA, and the consumption of margarine over the same time period. Clearly, margarine has a negative effect on marriages!
